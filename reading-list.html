It Ends with Us

It Ends with Us

Colleen Hoover

Romance Contemporary

Core Story:

Lily Bloom meets Ryle Kincaid, a charming neurosurgeon, and they fall in love. However, his volatile temper surfaces, forcing Lily to confront the painful cycle of domestic violence she witnessed in her childhood. The reappearance of her first love, Atlas, complicates everything.

Key Themes:

Domestic violence, emotional abuse, difficult choices, generational trauma, first love, and empowerment.

Why It Matters:

A heart-wrenching, powerful portrayal of the complexity of abusive relationships and the strength it takes to leave someone you love. Inspired by the author's own family story.

Published: August 2, 2016

It Starts with Us

It Starts with Us

Colleen Hoover

Romance Sequel

Core Story:

This sequel picks up after Book 1's epilogue. Lily and ex-husband Ryle co-parent their daughter civilly. When Lily unexpectedly runs into Atlas Corrigan, she finally feels the timing is right for a second chance at love. Told from both perspectives.

Key Themes:

Second chances, co-parenting after divorce/abuse, healing, moving forward, and Atlas's perspective.

Why It Matters:

A stunning sequel that provides the satisfying happy ending fans wanted. Offers deeper insight into Atlas's past while Lily navigates her rekindled romance.

Published: October 18, 2022

Pride & Prejudice

Pride & Prejudice

Jane Austen

Classic Romance

Core Story:

Elizabeth Bennet, one of five unmarried daughters in early 19th-century England, navigates social pressures and her mother's obsession with finding wealthy husbands. The story focuses on her developing relationship with the wealthy, proud Fitzwilliam Darcy. Their initial mutual dislike—his pride and her prejudice—must be overcome through dramatic events and self-realization.

Key Themes:

Pride and prejudice as blinding flaws, love vs. financial necessity in marriage, social class and reputation, family dynamics, and gender roles.

Why It Matters:

A timeless classic novel of manners and social satire. Originally titled "First Impressions" and published anonymously in 1813. One of the most beloved works of English literature.

Published: January 28, 1813

Sherlock Holmes Collection

Sir Arthur Conan Doyle

Mystery Classic

Famous Novels:

A Study in Scarlet (1887) - Introduces Holmes and Watson at Baker Street
The Sign of Four (1890) - Mysterious treasure and complex plot
The Hound of the Baskervilles (1902) - The most famous novel featuring a supernatural beast on Dartmoor

The legendary detective whose powers of deduction and logic revolutionized mystery fiction. An iconic literary figure whose influence extends far beyond literature into popular culture worldwide.

Published: 1887-1927
